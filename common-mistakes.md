## 🔴 High-Priority Recurring Mistakes

### 1. Article Omission 冠詞省略 (a / an / the)

**Problem:** 中文沒有冠詞，所以常常忘記加。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | Context |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| "I am software engineer" | "I am **a** software engineer" | 職業前要加 a |
| "go to office" | "go to **the** office" | 特定地點用 the |
| "My goal is to become staff engineer" | "My goal is to become **a** staff engineer" | 職業前加 a |
| "I work in small team" | "I work in **a** small team" | 可數名詞單數前加 a |

**Rule of thumb:** Every time you say a **job title**, pause — did you put "a" before it?

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q1)*

---

### 2. Subject Omission 主詞省略 (I)

**Problem:** 中文會省略主詞，英文不能。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| "easy say is busy" | "basically **I'm** busy" |
| "Have a meeting tomorrow" | "**I** have a meeting tomorrow" |
| "Going to the gym later" | "**I'm** going to the gym later" |

**Rule:** Every English sentence needs a subject. If there's no other subject, start with "I".

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q1)*

---

### 3. Preposition of Time (at / on / in)

**Problem:** 介系詞選錯，尤其是 "at Friday" 這種。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | Why |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| "at Friday" | "**on** Friday" | 星期用 on |
| "in weekend" | "**on** the weekend" (AmE) | 週末用 on |
| "on today" | just "**today**" | today 不用 preposition |
| "at 2026" | "**in** 2026" | 年份用 in |
| "on 6 PM" | "**at** 6 PM" | 精確時間用 at |

**Quick memory:** AT (zoom in) → ON (mid) → IN (zoom out)

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q2)*

See details: [../grammar-reference/prepositions-of-time.md](../grammar-reference/prepositions-of-time.md)

### 4. Chinese → English Direct Translation 中式英文

**Problem:** 把中文字字翻譯過來，結果英文不自然。

| ❌ Direct Translation | ✅ Natural English | 中文 |
|----------------------|-------------------|------|
| "easy say" | "basically" / "in short" | 簡單來說 |
| "goto" (one word) | "go to" (two words) | 去 |
| "take my kids go to school" | "take my kids to school" | 帶小孩去學校 |
| "do work" | "work" | 做工作 |
| "four days in weekend" | "four times a week" | 一週四次 |

**Rule:** When translating Chinese to English, think about the **concept**, not the individual words.

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q1, Q2)*

### 5. "Take" Already Means "帶去" — Don't Add "Go"

**Problem:** "take someone to a place" 已經完整表達「帶某人去某處」，不需要再加 go。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct | 中文 |
|---------|-----------|------|
| "take my kids go to school" | "take my kids to school" | 帶小孩去學校 |
| "take my wife go to the restaurant" | "take my wife to the restaurant" | 帶太太去餐廳 |
| "take my dog go to the park" | "take my dog to the park" | 帶狗去公園 |

**Pattern:** `take + someone + to + a place` (no "go"!)

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q1)*

---

### 6. "do work" is Redundant

**Problem:** "work" 本身就是動詞，不需要 "do" 。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| "I go to the office to do work" | "I go to the office **to work**" |
| "I need to do work now" | "I need to **work** now" |

**Exception:** "do the work" (with "the" + context) is OK in some cases, but `to work` is almost always cleaner.

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q1)*

### 7. Frequency Pattern: "N times a week"

**Problem:** 常錯在 "in a week" 或 "on week"。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| "four times in week" | "four times **a** week" |
| "four times on week" | "four times **a** week" |
| "four times in a week" | "four times **a** week" |
| "three times per the day" | "three times **a** day" / "three times **per** day" |

**Pattern:** `數字 + times + a + 時間單位`
- Use **a**, NOT **in** or **on**
- Formal: can use **per** instead of **a**

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q2)*

See details: [../grammar-reference/frequency-expressions.md](../grammar-reference/frequency-expressions.md)

### 8. "By + V-ing" for Method/Means

**Problem:** 忘記用 V-ing，或是列表時形式不一致。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| "I unwind by watch Netflix" | "I unwind by **watching** Netflix" |
| "I learn by practice" | "I learn by **practicing**" |
| "by watching, play, sing" (mixed) | "by **watching**, **playing**, **singing**" (all V-ing) |

**Rule:** After **by**, always use **V-ing**. All items in the list must match.

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q3)*

See details: [../grammar-reference/by-ving-and-parallel-structure.md](../grammar-reference/by-ving-and-parallel-structure.md)

### 9. List Punctuation (three items = two commas)

**Problem:** 列舉三樣東西時，常常漏掉第二個逗號（在 and/or 前面）。

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| "A, B and C" *(no Oxford comma)* | "A**,** B**,** and C" |
| "A, B or C" *(no Oxford comma)* | "A**,** B**,** or C" |
| "watching Netflix, playing badminton or singing" | "watching Netflix**,** playing badminton**,** or singing" |

**Rule:** 3 items → 2 commas. Always use the Oxford comma before "and"/"or".

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q3)*

---

### 10. "and" vs "or" — Choose Based on Meaning

| Situation | Connector |
|-----------|-----------|
| 全部都做/都是 | **and** |
| 其中選一個/輪流 | **or** |

Don't mix! If listing 3 items, use **one** connector consistently.

- ❌ "watching Netflix **and** playing badminton **or** singing" (mixed)
- ✅ "watching Netflix**,** playing badminton**,** **or** singing" (all "or")

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q3)*

---

### 11. Comma Splice — Don't Join Two Sentences with Just a Comma

**Problem:** 用逗號把兩個完整句子連在一起。

❌ **Wrong:**
> "I go to the office to work, basically I'm busy."
> (Two complete sentences joined by just a comma)

✅ **Correct options:**
1. Use a period: "I go to the office to work**. B**asically, I'm busy."
2. Use "so": "I go to the office to work**, so** basically I'm busy."
3. Use a dash: "I go to the office to work **—** basically, I'm busy."

*Last seen: 2026-04-19 (Q1)*

## 🟡 Medium-Priority Patterns to Watch

### 12. Word Order with Time Words

- ❌ "My today schedule" / "Today my schedule"
- ✅ "**Today**, I..." (beginning) OR "I... **today**" (end)

### 13. Parallel Structure in Lists

When listing items, they must have the **same grammatical form**:
- All nouns: apples, bananas, oranges ✅
- All V-ing: reading, writing, speaking ✅
- All infinitives: to read, to write, to speak ✅
- All adjectives: smart, kind, beautiful ✅

❌ Don't mix: "smart, kind, and has beauty"
✅ "smart, kind, and beautiful"

### 14. Standalone Subordinate Clauses

Don't start a sentence with "Because..." without a main clause.
- ❌ "Because you need to be organized."
- ✅ "**It's important because** you need to be organized."
- ✅ "Because you need to be organized**, it's important.**"

### 15. Present Simple vs. Other Tenses

Use Present Simple for habits and routines:
- ✅ "I **wake up** at 7 AM." (habit)
- ✅ "I **work** remotely four times a week." (routine)

## 🎯 Pre-Practice Checklist

Before speaking, scan this mental checklist:

```
□ Subject (usually "I") — Did I include it?
□ Article (a / an / the) — Before every job title and specific noun?
□ Preposition of time — Correct at/on/in?
□ No Chinese-style direct translation?
□ If listing — same form for each item? Enough commas?
□ If using "by" — V-ing form?
□ If joining sentences — comma splice? Use "so" or period!
```
