# "By + V-ing" and Parallel Structure in Lists

> Two closely related patterns that often appear together.

---

## 🎯 Pattern 1: By + V-ing 「用某方式做某事」

### The Rule

> When describing **how** you do something (the method/means), use:
>
> **by + verb-ing**

### Examples

| Sentence | 中文 |
|----------|------|
| I relax **by reading** books. | 我藉由看書來放鬆。 |
| I wake up **by drinking** coffee. | 我藉由喝咖啡醒來。 |
| I unwind **by watching** Netflix. | 我藉由看 Netflix 放鬆。 |
| I improve my English **by practicing** every day. | 我藉由每天練習來改善英文。 |
| I manage my schedule **by using** Google Calendar. | 我藉由用 Google Calendar 管理行程。 |
| I stay healthy **by exercising** regularly. | 我藉由規律運動保持健康。 |

### ⚠️ Why "V-ing" not "V"?

In English, after the preposition **"by"**, you need a **gerund** (動名詞 = V-ing form).

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|---------|-----------|
| by watch Netflix | by **watching** Netflix |
| by play badminton | by **playing** badminton |
| by sing | by **singing** |
| by read books | by **reading** books |

### 📝 Formula

```
Main clause + by + V-ing + (object/details)
 └─ Do what ─┘   └── How you do it ──┘
```

## 🎯 Pattern 2: Parallel Structure 平行結構

### The Rule

> When you list multiple items in a sentence, they must all have the **same grammatical form**.

### Why It Matters

Parallel structure makes sentences:
- ✨ Clearer to read
- ✨ More professional
- ✨ Easier to understand
- ✨ Rhythmically smooth

### Examples — V-ing List

If one item is V-ing, they all must be V-ing:

| ❌ Not Parallel | ✅ Parallel |
|----------------|------------|
| watching Netflix, play badminton, sing | **watching** Netflix, **playing** badminton, **singing** |
| cooking, cleaning, to do laundry | **cooking**, **cleaning**, **doing** laundry |
| reading, running, swim | **reading**, **running**, **swimming** |

### Examples — Noun List

| ❌ Not Parallel | ✅ Parallel |
|----------------|------------|
| I like apples, bananas, and to eat pizza. | I like **apples**, **bananas**, and **pizza**. |

### Examples — Infinitive (to + V) List

| ❌ Not Parallel | ✅ Parallel |
|----------------|------------|
| I want to read, to run, and swimming. | I want **to read**, **to run**, and **to swim**. |

### Examples — Adjective List

| ❌ Not Parallel | ✅ Parallel |
|----------------|------------|
| She is smart, kind, and has beauty. | She is **smart**, **kind**, and **beautiful**. |

---

## 🔗 How the Two Patterns Work Together

When you use **by + V-ing** with a list, **every item must be V-ing**:

### ❌ Wrong

> I unwind by watch**ing** Netflix, **play** badminton, or **sing**.

(混合了 V-ing, 原形, 原形 — 不一致)

### ✅ Correct

> I unwind by watch**ing** Netflix, play**ing** badminton, or sing**ing**.

(三個都是 V-ing — 一致)

## 📝 List Punctuation 標點符號

When listing 3+ items, use this format:

> **A , B , and/or C**

### ⚠️ Three items = TWO commas!

**✅ Correct patterns:**
- watching Netflix**,** playing badminton**,** or singing
- reading**,** writing**,** and speaking
- coffee**,** tea**,** or juice

**❌ Wrong patterns:**
- watching Netflix**,** playing badminton or singing *(missing comma before "or")*
- watching Netflix playing badminton or singing *(missing commas entirely — hard to read!)*

### "and" vs "or" — 怎麼選？

| Connector | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **and** | 全部都做/都是 | I eat rice, chicken, **and** vegetables. (全部都吃) |
| **or** | 選其中一個/輪流 | I drink coffee, tea, **or** water. (看心情選) |

**Choose "or" when:**
- You'll pick one option
- You'll alternate (輪流)
- You do different ones different times

**Choose "and" when:**
- You do all of them together
- All items are true at the same time

### The Oxford Comma 🎓

The comma **before "and"/"or"** in a list is called the **Oxford comma**. It's considered good practice in formal writing:

- ✅ "A, B**,** and C" (with Oxford comma — clearer)
- ✅ "A, B and C" (without Oxford comma — also acceptable in British English)

**My recommendation:** **Use the Oxford comma** — it prevents ambiguity.

## 🎯 Combining Everything — Real Examples

### Example 1 (My session answer)

> I unwind **by watching** Netflix**,** **playing** badminton**,** **or** singing.

Analysis:
- ✅ "by" → V-ing triggered
- ✅ All three actions in V-ing form (watching / playing / singing)
- ✅ Two commas (one after Netflix, one after badminton)
- ✅ "or" chosen because I pick among these

### Example 2

> I start my day **by drinking** coffee**,** **checking** my emails**,** **and** **planning** my tasks.

Analysis:
- ✅ "by" → V-ing
- ✅ Three actions, all V-ing
- ✅ "and" because I do all three every morning

### Example 3

> At home, I usually do household chores like **cleaning**, **washing** dishes, **cooking**, **and** **doing** laundry.

Analysis:
- ✅ Four V-ing items
- ✅ Commas between each
- ✅ "and" before last item (I do all of these)

## 🧪 Quick Self-Check Questions

Before you finalize a list sentence, ask:

1. ✅ **Do all items have the same form?** (all V-ing, all nouns, all infinitives, etc.)
2. ✅ **Did I use "by" → then all verbs must be V-ing?**
3. ✅ **Do I have the right number of commas?** (3 items = 2 commas)
4. ✅ **Did I choose "and" (all) or "or" (selection)?**
5. ✅ **Is there a comma before "and"/"or"?** (Oxford comma)

## ❌ Common Mistakes for Me

Based on session logs:

1. ❌ **Mixing V forms in lists:**
   - "watching Netflix, play badminton, singing" → inconsistent
   - ✅ "watching Netflix, playing badminton, singing"

2. ❌ **Missing comma before "or"/"and":**
   - "watching Netflix, playing badminton or singing"
   - ✅ "watching Netflix, playing badminton**,** or singing"

3. ❌ **"by" + base verb:**
   - "by watch Netflix"
   - ✅ "by **watching** Netflix"

4. ❌ **Mixing "and" with "or":**
   - "watching Netflix and playing badminton or singing" → awkward logic
   - ✅ Pick one connector for one logical relationship
