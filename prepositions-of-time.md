# Prepositions of Time: at / on / in

> Quick reference for which time preposition to use.

---

## 🎯 The Core Rule

| Preposition | Use for | Precision |
|-------------|---------|-----------|
| **at** | Specific clock times, moments | 最精確 ⏰ |
| **on** | Days and dates | 中間 📅 |
| **in** | Months, years, seasons, longer periods | 最廣 📆 |

**Think of it as a "zoom level":** at (zoom in) → on (mid) → in (zoom out)

---

## ⏰ AT — Specific Time Points

Use for exact clock times and specific moments.

| Example | 中文 |
|---------|------|
| at 7 AM | 早上七點 |
| at 6:00 PM | 晚上六點 |
| at noon | 中午 |
| at midnight | 午夜 |
| at night | 在晚上 |
| at lunchtime | 午餐時間 |
| at the weekend (British) | 週末 |

**Full sentences:**
- I wake up **at** 7 AM.
- I have lunch **at** 12 noon.
- I disconnect from work **at** 6 PM.
- I go to bed **at** midnight.

---

## 📅 ON — Days and Dates

Use for specific days of the week, dates, and specific days/parts of specific days.

| Example | 中文 |
|---------|------|
| on Friday | 星期五 |
| on Monday morning | 週一早上 |
| on April 5th | 四月五日 |
| on New Year's Day | 元旦那天 |
| on my birthday | 我的生日那天 |
| on the weekend (American) | 週末 |
| on Christmas Day | 聖誕節那天 |

**Full sentences:**
- I go into the office **on** Friday.
- I was born **on** April 5th.
- We have a meeting **on** Monday morning.
- I rest **on** weekends.

---

## 📆 IN — Months, Years, Seasons, Long Periods

Use for broader time periods.

| Example | 中文 |
|---------|------|
| in April | 四月 |
| in 2026 | 2026 年 |
| in summer | 夏天 |
| in the morning | 在早上 |
| in the afternoon | 在下午 |
| in the evening | 在晚上 |
| in the 21st century | 21 世紀 |
| in two weeks | 兩週後 |

**Full sentences:**
- I start my career **in** 2020.
- It's hot **in** summer.
- I drink coffee **in** the morning.
- I'll see you **in** two weeks.

---

## ⚠️ Tricky Cases — Easy to Confuse!

### Morning/Afternoon/Evening — which preposition?

| With | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| Just "morning" | **in** | I work **in** the morning. |
| Specific day + morning | **on** | I have a meeting **on** Monday morning. |

### "Weekend" — different for British vs. American

| Region | Preposition | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| American English | **on** | I rest **on** the weekend. |
| British English | **at** | I rest **at** the weekend. |

**Kiddy's course uses American English** → use **on**.

### "Night"

| Form | Preposition | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| "at night" | **at** | I can't sleep **at** night. |
| "on Monday night" | **on** | I'll call **on** Monday night. |
| "in the night" | **in** (less common) | I woke up **in** the night. |

---

## 🚫 NO Preposition Needed

Don't use any preposition with:

| Word | Example |
|------|---------|
| today | I'm busy **today**. ❌ *on today* |
| tomorrow | See you **tomorrow**. ❌ *at tomorrow* |
| yesterday | I went **yesterday**. ❌ *on yesterday* |
| tonight | I'll call **tonight**. ❌ *at tonight* |
| this week / month / year | I'm traveling **this week**. ❌ *in this week* |
| last week / month / year | I saw him **last week**. |
| next week / month / year | The party is **next Friday**. |

---

## 🎯 Quick Decision Tree

```
什麼時候？

├─ 精確時間點 (時鐘上的數字、noon、midnight)
│  → AT
│
├─ 星期幾、日期、特定的某一天
│  → ON
│
├─ 月、年、季節、大段時間、morning/afternoon/evening (沒有搭配特定日期)
│  → IN
│
└─ today / tomorrow / yesterday / this X / last X / next X
   → 不用 preposition！
```
